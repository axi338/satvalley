Okay, here's an analysis of the SAT practice test answer key and explanation document, followed by a concise "Explanation Style Guide" based on the findings.

**Analysis of Answer Explanations**

**1. Structure:**

*   **Correct Answer First:** Each explanation begins by explicitly stating the correct answer choice (e.g., "Choice B is the best answer because...").
*   **Explanation of Correct Answer:**  The explanation then elaborates *why* the correct answer is indeed correct, directly relating it to the prompt and specific evidence/logic within the provided text/scenario. For Reading & Writing, this involves referring back to the passage, identifying key phrases, and demonstrating how the answer choice aligns with the text's meaning/purpose. For math, it entails a detailed step-by-step logical breakdown of the correct approach.
*   **Explanations of Incorrect Answers:** After explaining the correct answer, each incorrect answer choice is addressed individually. The explanation *why* the incorrect answer is wrong is explained, often identifying common mistakes in reasoning or connecting it to a misinterpretation of the text/scenario. The reasoning identifies the flaw and points out where the student may have gone astray.
*   **"Therefore..." Conclusion:**  Explanations for Math questions tend to conclude with a "Therefore..." statement which reiterates the correct answer by linking back to the prompt

**2. Tone:**

*   **Pedagogical:** The tone is educational and instructive.  The explanations are designed to help students learn from their mistakes and understand the underlying concepts.
*   **Relatively Formal:** While pedagogical, the language remains relatively formal and academic (i.e. avoids slang).
*   **Concise:** Explanations are detailed enough to be helpful but avoid excessive wordiness. The goal is clarity and understanding, not exhaustive analysis.

**3. Mathematical Notation:**

*   **LaTeX-Style/Equation Editor:** Mathematical equations and notation are rendered using what appears to be a tool equivalent to LaTeX. The format is typographically appropriate with symbols and mathematical fractions (avoiding in-line fractions or slash-based notation unless for the most basic operations).

**4. Keywords and Phrases:**

*   **Correct Answer Intro:** "Choice \[Letter] is the best answer because it...", "Choice \[Letter] is correct because..."
*   **Incorrect Answer Intro:** "Choice \[Letter] is incorrect because...", or "Choice \[Letter] is incorrect and may result from..." (followed by the possible error).
*   **Explanation Phrases (Reading & Writing):**
    *   "In this context, \[word] means..."
    *   "The text indicates that..."
    *   "This suggests that..."
    *   "...this context conveys..."
    *   "This contrasts with..."
    *   "The text does not suggest that..."
*   **Explanation Phrases (Math):**
    *   "It's given that..."
    *   "Substituting \[value] for \[variable]..."
    *   "Therefore..." (used frequently to connect steps in logic and conclude the explanation of correct and incorrect answers)
    *   "Which is equivalent to..."
    *   "It follows that..."
    *   "Dividing both sides..." / "Multiplying both sides..."/ Subtracting both sides..." etc.
    *   "Applying the distributive property..."

**Explanation Style Guide**

**General Principles:**

*   **Accuracy First:** Ensure all explanations are mathematically/logically sound and factually correct.
*   **Clarity is Key:** Use clear and concise language, avoiding jargon where possible. Aim for understanding, not just correctness.
*   **Focus on Reasoning:** Explain *why* answers are correct or incorrect, not just *what* the answer is.
*   **Emphasize Learning:** Frame explanations to help students identify and correct their own errors in thinking.
*   **Consistency:** Follow the specified structure and tone consistently across all explanations.

**Specific Guidelines:**

1.  **Structure:**
    *   Begin by stating the correct answer choice:  "Choice \[Letter] is the best answer because..." or "Choice \[Letter] is correct because..."
    *   Explain the correct answer thoroughly, providing a step-by-step breakdown of the reasoning or process to arrive at the correct answer. Refer to passage evidence (R&W) and perform calculations (Math).
    *   Address each incorrect answer individually: "Choice \[Letter] is incorrect because..." Explain *why* it is wrong.
    *   For Math questions, conclude with a "Therefore, the \[correct answer] is..." statement

2.  **Tone:**
    *   Pedagogical and instructive.
    *   Relatively formal language (avoid slang).
    *   Concise and to the point.

3.  **Mathematical Notation:**
    *   Use a tool with LaTeX-style equation editing for fractions, symbols, and mathematical expressions.
    *   Maintain proper mathematical typesetting.

4.  **Phrases to Use:**

    *   **Correct Answer Intro:**  "Choice \[Letter] is the best answer because...", "Choice \[Letter] is correct because..."
    *   **Incorrect Answer Intro:** "Choice \[Letter] is incorrect because...", or "Choice \[Letter] is incorrect and may result from..."
    *   **Reading & Writing:**
        *   "In this context, \[word] means..."
        *   "The text indicates that..."
        *   "This suggests that..."
        *   "...this context conveys..."

    *   **Math:**
        *   "It's given that..."
        *   "Substituting \[value] for \[variable]..."
        *   "Therefore..."
        *   "Which is equivalent to..."
        *   "It follows that..."
        *   "Dividing both sides..."

By adhering to this "Explanation Style Guide", you can generate answer explanations for SAT questions with a high degree of consistency and instructional value.
